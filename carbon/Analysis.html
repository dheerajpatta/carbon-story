Who emits the most Co2? What is each country's share of global Co2 emissions?

China, the USA and other developed nations account for more than half of global emissions leading the race of most emissions by country.

The distribution of emissions has changed significantly over time. The UK was – until 1888, when it was overtaken by the US – the world’s largest emitter. Later in 21st century Asia, especially China overtook US to be the largest emittor of Co2.
